# Chapter 10: Assessment of Nutrition Status and Nutrition Requirements

## Classification of Nutrition Status
*   **Starvation-Associated Malnutrition (Marasmus):** Prolonged inadequate intake, absorption, or utilization of protein and energy. Characterized by severe wasting of somatic protein (skeletal muscle) and adipose tissue (subcutaneous fat). Visceral protein (albumin, transferrin) production is usually preserved.
*   **Chronic Disease-Related Malnutrition:** Mild-to-moderate inflammation, duration >3 months. Causes: organ failure, pancreatic cancer, rheumatoid arthritis, cystic fibrosis, sarcopenic obesity, chronic lung disease, inflammatory bowel disease, malabsorption.
*   **Acute Disease- or Injury-Related Malnutrition:** Marked inflammation, duration <3 months. Causes: major infection, burns, trauma, traumatic brain injury.
*   **Obesity:** Body mass index (BMI) $\ge$30 kg/m$^2$. Severe obesity: BMI $\ge$40 kg/m$^2$. Childhood obesity: BMI $\ge$95th percentile for age and sex.

## Nutrition Screening
*   **Screening Triggers:** Recent weight loss of $\ge$5% to 10%; inadequate food intake for $\ge$1 to 2 weeks.
*   **Validated Tools:** Mini Nutritional Assessment—Short Form (MNA-SF), Malnutrition Screening Tool (MST), Nutrition Risk Screening 2002 (NRS 2002), Nutrition Risk in the Critically Ill (NUTRIC).

## Nutrition Assessment

### Pertinent Data from a Nutrition-Focused Medical, Surgical, and Dietary History (Table 10-1)
*   **Nutrition Intake and Dietary Habits:** Anorexia, unusual/absent taste, diet (vegetarian/vegan), enteral/parenteral nutrition, supplemental vitamin/mineral/herbal intake, food allergies/intolerances.
*   **Underlying Pathology with Nutritional Effects:** Chronic infection/inflammatory state, cancer, endocrine disorders, chronic illness (lung disease, cirrhosis, kidney failure), hypermetabolic states (trauma, burns, sepsis), digestive/absorptive disease, nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, constipation, hyperlipidemia.
*   **End-Organ Effects:** Weight changes, skin/hair changes, exercise intolerance/fatigue, GI tract symptoms.
*   **Gastrointestinal Surgery:** Bariatric surgery, small bowel/colon resection or diversion, gastrectomy.
*   **Miscellaneous:** Catabolic medications/therapies (corticosteroids, immunosuppressive agents, radiation, chemotherapy), other medications (diuretics, laxatives, antipsychotics, anabolic steroids), genetics, alcohol/drug misuse, pregnancy/lactation.

### Nutrition-Focused Physical Examination (NFPE) Findings Suggestive of Malnutrition (Table 10-3)
*   **General Inspection:** Edema, cachexia/obesity, signs of dehydration (poor skin turgor, sunken eyes, orthostasis, dry mucous membranes), muscle wasting, loss of subcutaneous fat.
*   **Vital Signs:** Fever, tachycardia.
*   **Skin:** Thin, shiny, dry, scaly, dermatitis, rash, decubitus ulcers, poorly healing wounds, bruising, petechiae, follicular hyperkeratosis, pallor, cyanosis, jaundice.
*   **Nails:** Spoon shape, clubbing, transverse ridging/banding, mottled, pale, poor capillary refill.
*   **Head/Hair:** Dry, dull, brittle, sparse, easily pluckable, corkscrew, coiled, depigmented, alopecia, scaly/flaky scalp, moon face, bilateral temporal wasting.
*   **Eyes/Nose:** Dull dry appearance to sclerae/inner lids, dull milky cornea, Bitot’s spots (vitamin A deficiency), icteric sclerae, pale conjunctiva, cracked red corners of eyes.
*   **Mouth:** Pale/red/bleeding gums, dry/cracked/red lips, fissures at corners (angular stomatitis), vertical cracks of lips (cheilosis), magenta/beefy red tongue (glossitis), atrophied papillae, excessive dental caries.
*   **Neck/Chest:** Enlarged thyroid, distended neck veins, prominent bony chest, visible ribs.
*   **Abdomen:** Scaphoid, concave, protuberant, hepatomegaly, ascites.
*   **Musculoskeletal:** Ataxia, numbness, tingling, swollen/painful joints, rickets, knock knees, bowleg.

## Anthropometric Measurements

### Ideal Body Weight (IBW) Equations (Devine)
*   **Adult men:** IBW = 50 kg + (2.3 × inches over 5 feet)
*   **Adult women:** IBW = 45.5 kg + (2.3 × inches over 5 feet)
*   *Note for SI units (cm):* Inches over 5 feet = 0.394 × (height in cm − 152).
*   **Adjusted Body Weight (AdjBW):** AdjBW = ([ABW − IBW] × 0.25) + IBW *(Note: Use is generally not recommended for metabolic rate equations).*
*   **Children (<18 years, <60 inches) Traub Equation:** IBW = ([height (cm)]$^2$ × 1.65) / 1,000

### Stature Estimation Equations
*   **From Demispan:**
    *   Women: Height (cm) = (1.35 × demispan [cm]) + 60.1
    *   Men: Height (cm) = (1.4 × demispan [cm]) + 57.8
*   **From Knee Height:**
    *   Women: Height (cm) = [1.83 × knee height (cm)] − [0.24 × age (yr)] + 84.8
    *   Men: Height (cm) = [2.02 × knee height (cm)] − [0.04 × age (yr)] + 64.9

## Laboratory Assessment

### Assessment of Serum Visceral Proteins (Table 10-6)
| Serum Protein | Half-Life (Days) | Functions | Factors Resulting in Increased Values | Factors Resulting in Decreased Values |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Albumin** | 18-20 | Maintains plasma oncotic pressure; transports small molecules | Dehydration, anabolic steroids, insulin, infection | Fluid overload; edema; kidney dysfunction; nephrotic syndrome; poor dietary intake; impaired digestion; burns; heart failure; cirrhosis; thyroid, adrenal, or pituitary hormones; trauma; sepsis |
| **Transferrin** | 8-9 | Binds Fe in plasma; transports Fe to bone | Fe deficiency, pregnancy, hypoxia, chronic blood loss, estrogens | Chronic infection, cirrhosis, burns, enteropathies, nephrotic syndrome, cortisone, testosterone |
| **Prealbumin (transthyretin)** | 2-3 | Binds T$_3$ and T$_4$; retinol-binding protein carrier | Impaired kidney function | Cirrhosis, hepatitis, stress, surgery, inflammation, hyperthyroidism, cystic fibrosis, burns, zinc deficiency |

*   **Transferrin (TFN) Estimation:** TFN (mg/dL) = (total iron-binding capacity [mcg/dL] × 0.8) − 43
*   **Nitrogen Balance:**
    *   Nitrogen output (g/day) $\approx$ 24-hour UUN + 4
    *   Alternative: 24-hour UUN + 2 + 20% of urinary urea losses.
    *   If TUN is used: TUN + 1.05.
*   **Immune Function:** Total lymphocyte count (TLC) < 1,200 cells/mm$^3$ is a nonspecific marker for nutrition depletion.

## Nutrient Requirements

### Energy Requirements (kcal/kg method)
*   **Healthy, normal nutrition status, minimal illness severity:** 20-25 kcal ABW/kg/day
*   **Illness, metabolic stress (first 7-10 days of ICU stay):** 12-25 kcal ABW/kg/day
*   **Illness, metabolic stress (BMI <30 kg/m$^2$):** 25-30 kcal ABW/kg/day
*   **Illness, metabolic stress (BMI $\ge$30 kg/m$^2$):** 11-14 kcal ABW/kg/day OR 22-25 kcal IBW/kg/day
*   **Major burn ($\ge$50% TBSA):** 25 kcal/kg ABW + 40 kcal per % TBSA burned (adult) OR 25-35 kcal/kg/day in nonobese patients and 21 kcal/kg/day in obese patients.

### Equations to Estimate Energy Expenditure in Adults (Table 10-10)
*   **Harris-Benedict Equations (kcal/day):**
    *   Men: BEE = 66 + (13.75W + 5H [cm]) – (6.8A)
    *   Women: BEE = 655 + (9.6W + 1.8H [cm]) – (4.7A)
*   **Mifflin-St. Jeor Equations (kcal/day):**
    *   Men: 10W + 6.25H (cm) – 5A + 5
    *   Women: 10W + 6.25H (cm) – 5A – 161
*   **Penn State Equation (kcal/day) (Critically Ill Adults):**
    *   Mifflin(0.96) + Tmax(167) + Ve(31) – 6212
    *   *Modified for age $\ge$60 yr with BMI $\ge$30 kg/m$^2$:* Mifflin(0.71) + Tmax(85) + Ve(64) – 3085
*   **Measured Resting Energy Expenditure (MREE) via Indirect Calorimetry:**
    *   MREE = ([3.94 VO$_2$ + 1.11 VCO$_2$] + [2.17 uN$_2$]) × 1.44
    *   *Respiratory Quotient (RQ):* VCO$_2$/VO$_2$. Fat = 0.71; Carbohydrate = 1; Protein = 0.82; Mixed = 0.85. RQ > 1 indicates overfeeding.

### Stress Factors for Use in Adults (Table 10-12)
*   **No Stress:** Confined to bed (1.2); Out of bed/normal activity (1.3)
*   **Mild Stress:** Postoperative recovery (1-1.15); Trauma: mild (1.2)
*   **Moderate Stress:** Sepsis: moderate (1.2-1.4); Trauma: CNS sedated (1.3); Trauma: moderate to severe (1.3-1.4)
*   **Severe Stress:** Sepsis: severe (1.3); Trauma: CNS severe (Up to 1.3); Burns (Up to 2.0)

### Protein Requirements
*   **Healthy Adults:** 0.8 g/kg/day
*   **Adults >60 years:** 1.5 g/kg/day
*   **Metabolic Stress:** 1.5 to 2 g/kg/day
*   **Critically Ill:** 1.2 to 2 g/kg/day
*   **Obese Critically Ill:** 2 g/kg IBW (BMI 30-40 kg/m$^2$); $\ge$2.5 g/kg IBW (BMI >40 kg/m$^2$)
*   **Significant Burns:** 2.5 to 3 g/kg ABW/day
*   **Liver Failure:** 0.5 g/kg/day (unless hypercatabolic, then 1.5 g/kg/day)
*   **Pregnancy/Lactation:** 1.1 g/kg/day (or 6-10 g/day above usual RDA)

### Fat and Fiber Requirements
*   **Fat:** 10% to 35% of total calories in adults. Children 1-3 years: 30-40%; Children 4-18 years: 25-35%.
*   **Fiber:** Adults $\le$50 years: 38 g/day (men), 25-26 g/day (women). Adults >50 years: 30 g/day (men), 21 g/day (women).

### Fluid Requirements
*   **Adults:** 30 to 40 mL/kg
*   **Children (Holliday-Segar Method):**
    *   First 10 kg: 100 mL/kg/day
    *   11-20 kg: +50 mL/kg/day
    *   >20 kg: +20 mL/kg/day

## Drug–Nutrient Interactions (Table 10-16)
| Medication | Effect |
| :--- | :--- |
| **ACE Inhibitors / ARBs** | Increased urinary zinc losses |
| **Antacids** | Thiamine deficiency |
| **Antibiotics** | Vitamin K deficiency |
| **Aspirin** | Folic acid deficiency; increased vitamin C excretion |
| **Cathartics** | Increased requirements for vitamins D, C, and B$_6$ |
| **Cholestyramine / Colestipol** | Vitamins A, D, E, and K and $\beta$-carotene malabsorption |
| **Corticosteroids** | Decreased vitamins A, D, and C |
| **Diuretics (loop)** | Thiamine deficiency |
| **Diuretics (thiazides)** | Increased urinary zinc losses |
| **Efavirenz** | Vitamin D deficiency |
| **H2 Antagonists / PPIs** | Vitamin B$_{12}$ malabsorption (reduced acid impairs release from food); PPIs also decrease iron absorption |
| **Isoniazid** | Vitamin B$_6$ and niacin deficiency |
| **Isotretinoin** | Vitamin A increases toxicity |
| **Mercaptopurine** | Niacin deficiency |
| **Methotrexate** | Folic acid inhibits effect |
| **Orlistat** | Vitamins A, D, E, and K malabsorption |
| **Pentamidine / Primidone / Trimethoprim** | Folic acid deficiency/depletion |
| **Phenobarbital / Phenytoin** | Increased vitamin D metabolism; Phenytoin also decreases folic acid |
| **Protease inhibitors** | Vitamin D deficiency (impaired renal hydroxylation) |
| **Sulfasalazine** | Folic acid malabsorption |
| **Warfarin** | Vitamin K inhibits effect; vitamins A, C, and E may affect prothrombin time |
| **Valproic acid** | Zinc, carnitine deficiency |
| **Zidovudine** | Folic acid and B$_{12}$ deficiencies increase myelosuppression |

*Note: IV lipid emulsions used as vehicles for propofol (1.1 kcal/mL for 10%) and clevidipine (2.0 kcal/mL for 20%) contribute fat calories and must be accounted for to avoid overfeeding.*
