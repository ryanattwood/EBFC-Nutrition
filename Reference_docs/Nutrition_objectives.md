Describe appropriate methods to assess AND calculate a patient’s nutritional requirements

  - List the methods by which clinicians assess nutritional needs of patients
  - Describe the utility of each method of nutritional assessment
  - Be able to calculate nutritional needs based on a given patient case

Design a patient-specific parenteral nutrition (PN) formula, including macronutrient and micronutrient content

  - List indications for the use of parenteral nutrition (PN) in adults
  - Compare the advantages and disadvantages of using PN versus EN
  - Describe the importance of each macronutrient and micronutrient
  - Calculate a patient’s daily fluid requirements
  - Determine calcium phosphate solubility of a PN solution
  - Discuss important considerations for the administration of PN
  - Explain the differences between continuous and cyclic or intermittent
    infusions of PN
  - List medications that are compatible with PN solutions
  - List advantages of central and peripheral administration and determine which
    method is best for a given patient case

Perform calculations necessary to successfully compound PN formulas, including volume of macro- and micro-nutrients, caloric content, osmolarity, solubility, and infusion rate

  - Calculate macronutrient content using grams, kcals, and concentration of a
    given PN solution
  - Given a PN order and the concentration of the stock solutions and additives,
    calculate the amount of each additive needed for the admixture
  - Given a total volume, calculate an administration rate
  - Calculate the volume of sterile water needed to QS the PN solution to the
    total volume ordered

Discuss complications of PN and list recommendations for preventing and treating these complications

  - Recall mechanical, technical, metabolic, and infectious complications of PN
    therapy
  - Recall ways to avoid the occurrence of these complications
  - Explain compatibility considerations for PN solutions

Describe optimal enteral nutrition (EN) feeding routes based on patient needs

  - List the indications for the use of enteral nutrition (EN) in adults
  - Recommend an appropriate route and access methods for EN support
  - Understand the various types of feeding tubes for EN
  - Given a total volume, calculate an administration rate
  - Compare and contrast the different administration methods for EN
    (continuous, intermittent, cyclic, bolus)
  - Differentiate between short-term access and long-term access methods
  - Compare the advantages and disadvantages of using EN versus PN
