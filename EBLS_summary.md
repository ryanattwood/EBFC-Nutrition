# OPERATIONAL FRAMEWORK: EVIDENCE-BASED LEARNING STRATEGIES (EBLS)

## SYSTEM ARCHITECTURE: S.A.L.A.M.I. PROTOCOL
*(Systematic Approach to Learning and Metacognitive Improvement)*

### CORE COGNITIVE MECHANISMS
*   **Active Recall (Retrieval Practice):** Accessing information from memory without external cues.
*   **Spaced Repetition:** Distributing retrieval practice over sequential, daily intervals to introduce desirable difficulty via controlled forgetting.
*   **Interleaved Practice:** Alternating related subject matter within a single study session to force contextual neural mapping.
*   **Elaboration:** Translating concepts into proprietary language and generating relational connections.
*   **Metacognitive Monitoring:** Generating quantitative data during study to calibrate actual knowledge versus perceived knowledge (Judgments of Learning).

---

## PHASE 0: PRE-EXECUTION SETUP (ENVIRONMENT & PRODUCTIVITY)

### 0.1 Environmental Parameters
*   **Location Criteria:** High safety, high accessibility, moderate comfort, zero clutter.
*   **Distraction Protocol:** 
    *   `IF` study session initiated, `THEN` disable all non-essential digital notifications.
    *   `IF` human distractors present, `THEN` relocate or deploy noise-canceling protocols.

### 0.2 Temporal Parameters
*   **Macro-Scheduling:** Map all semester deadlines, exams, and fixed commitments.
*   **Micro-Scheduling:** Allocate minimum 20 hours/week for independent study.
*   **Biological Maintenance:** Mandate >7 hours of sleep per 24-hour cycle.

---

## PHASE 1: PRECLASS PREPARATION (PRIMING)
**Objective:** Reactivate prior knowledge and map cognitive load.
**Timing:** 10–20 minutes prior to lecture.

### 1.1 Execution Steps
1.  **Identify Targets:** Extract primary topics from syllabus, slide titles, or reading headers.
2.  **Execute Focused Listing:** Brainstorm and list all preexisting knowledge related to the target topics from memory. (Do not verify accuracy yet).
3.  **Analyze Learning Objectives:** 
    *   `IF` objective uses "List/Define/Identify", `THEN` prepare for Factual Knowledge encoding.
    *   `IF` objective uses "Explain/Compare/Contrast", `THEN` prepare for Conceptual Knowledge encoding.
    *   `IF` objective uses "Create/Assess/Derive", `THEN` prepare for Procedural/Application encoding.
4.  **Map Cognitive Load:** Scan materials for high-density diagrams, novel vocabulary, or complex formulas. Flag for high-attention focus during class.
5.  **Isolate Vetted Resources:** Restrict pre-study to instructor-provided materials only.

---

## PHASE 2: IN-CLASS ENGAGEMENT (CONTEXTUALIZATION)
**Objective:** Filter sensory input and initiate primary encoding.
**Timing:** Duration of lecture/class.

### 2.1 Note-Taking Logic
*   **Rule 1:** Zero dictation. Do not transcribe verbatim.
*   **Rule 2:** Paraphrase and summarize instructor input.
*   **Rule 3:** Dynamic Density Adjustment:
    *   `IF` Factual Knowledge + Instructor provides slides, `THEN` take minimal/zero notes.
    *   `IF` Conceptual Knowledge (analogies, relationships), `THEN` capture explanatory logic.
    *   `IF` High Cognitive Load (dense diagrams/rapid delivery), `THEN` cease writing, focus on auditory comprehension, and mark timestamp for post-class audio review.

---

## PHASE 3: POSTCLASS REVIEW (POSTVIEWING)
**Objective:** Bridge contextualization to memory consolidation.
**Timing:** 10–20 minutes, executed on the *same day* as the lecture.

### 3.1 Execution Steps
1.  **Execute 1-Minute Paper / Focused Listing:** 
    *   Close all materials.
    *   Set timer for 60 seconds.
    *   Write summary of core concepts from memory.
2.  **Audit Notes:** Open materials. Compare memory output to actual notes. Correct errors and fill gaps.
3.  **Flag High-Friction Items:** Mark complex tables/diagrams requiring extended Phase 4 processing.
4.  **Escalate Unresolved Confusion:** List specific concepts not understood. Schedule instructor/tutor intervention.
5.  **Locate Application Targets:** Identify practice problems related to the day's concepts.

---

## PHASE 4: PREEXAM PREPARATION (ENCODING & CONSOLIDATION)
**Objective:** Build durable working knowledge via spaced, interleaved active recall.
**Timing:** Daily scheduled blocks.

### 4.1 Session Architecture (S.M.A.R.T. Logic)
1.  **Specific:** Define exact page ranges or problem numbers.
2.  **Measurable:** Define success metric (e.g., "Achieve 100% green boxes").
3.  **Attainable:** Calibrate volume to time limit (e.g., do not assign 60 pages to a 60-minute block).
4.  **Relevant:** Prioritize top 2-3 most difficult subjects.
5.  **Time-Bound:** Restrict blocks to 50 minutes maximum, followed by a 10-minute break.

### 4.2 Interleaving Protocol
*   `IF` studying Subject A (e.g., Pharmacology of Drug X), `THEN` follow immediately with Subject B (e.g., Therapeutics of Drug X).
*   `IF` practicing problem sets, `THEN` randomize problem types. Do not block-practice identical formulas.

### 4.3 Active Recall Toolset (Select & Rotate to Prevent Automaticity)
*   **Tool A: Empty Outline**
    *   Delete sub-bullets from digital notes.
    *   Fill blank spaces from memory.
    *   Verify against master notes.
*   **Tool B: Concept Mapping**
    *   Draw central node.
    *   Map relational nodes and connecting logic from memory.
    *   Verify against master notes.
*   **Tool C: Question Generation**
    *   Convert notes into How/Why/Compare questions.
    *   Answer from memory.
    *   Verify against master notes.
*   **Tool D: The Boxing Technique (Primary Data-Driven Tool)**
    1.  Overlay digital shapes (boxes) onto key slide/note data.
    2.  Type a retrieval cue (question) inside the box aligned with Bloom's taxonomy objectives.
    3.  Attempt recall from memory.
    4.  Move box to verify accuracy.
    5.  Apply Conditional Formatting (Feedback Loop):
        *   `IF` 0% recall, `THEN` color box **RED**.
        *   `IF` partial recall, `THEN` color box **YELLOW**.
        *   `IF` 100% recall, `THEN` color box **GREEN**.
    6.  *Iteration Rule:* Wait minimum 24 hours before re-testing. Target remaining RED/YELLOW boxes.

### 4.4 Application Practice Protocol
*   **Rule 1:** Initiate application practice immediately after initial encoding. Do not wait for "perfect" understanding.
*   **Rule 2:** Simulate Exam Conditions.
    *   `IF` practicing, `THEN` enforce time limits (60-90 seconds per question).
    *   `IF` practicing, `THEN` zero access to notes, peers, or answer keys.
*   **Rule 3:** Problem Deconstruction Algorithm.
    1.  Read final sentence first (isolate the core question).
    2.  List required concepts.
    3.  Extract relevant variables; discard distracting data.
    4.  Select required formula/guideline.

---

## PHASE 5: POSTASSESSMENT REVIEW (METACOGNITIVE CALIBRATION)
**Objective:** Extract performance data to optimize future S.A.L.A.M.I. execution.
**Timing:** Immediately upon exam return.

### 5.1 Exam Wrapper Algorithm
1.  **Audit Missed Questions:** Tally every incorrect answer.
2.  **Categorize Failure Point & Apply Corrective Action:**
    *   `IF` missed due to *Priming/Context* (e.g., skipped class, didn't understand initial lecture):
        *   `THEN` increase Phase 1 (Previewing) and Phase 3 (Postviewing) compliance. Escalate questions to instructor within 24 hours of lecture.
    *   `IF` missed due to *Memory* (e.g., blanked out, confused two concepts):
        *   `THEN` increase Phase 4 Active Recall frequency. Shift from passive reading to the Boxing Technique. Increase spacing intervals.
    *   `IF` missed due to *Utilization* (e.g., knew the facts but couldn't solve the case):
        *   `THEN` increase Phase 4 Application Practice. Enforce strict "no-notes" timed simulation during problem sets.
    *   `IF` missed due to *Exam Skills* (e.g., misread question, ran out of time):
        *   `THEN` implement reading protocols (read stem first, highlight "EXCEPT/ALWAYS"). Enforce 60-90 second time limits during practice.
3.  **Assess Wellness Variables:**
    *   `IF` high anxiety or distraction impaired performance, `THEN` initiate clinical/counseling support or request formal testing accommodations.
