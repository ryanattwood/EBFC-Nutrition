# Chapter 11: Parenteral Nutrition

## Indications and Timing for Parenteral Nutrition

### Time Frame for Initiating PN (Table 11-1)
*   **Adults:**
    *   *Previously well-nourished:* Initiate PN after 7 days of oral intake or EN <50% of estimated requirements.
    *   *Nutritionally at-risk* (involuntary loss $\ge$10% UBW in 6 months; $\ge$5% UBW in 1 month; loss/gain of 10 lbs [4.5 kg] in 6 months; BMI <18.5 kg/m$^2$; inadequate intake >7 days): Initiate PN after 3-5 days of oral intake or EN <50% of estimated requirements.
    *   *Moderate or severely malnourished:* Initiate PN as soon as feasible if oral/EN is not possible or sufficient.
    *   *Metabolically unstable:* Delay PN until clinical condition improves.
*   **Neonates:**
    *   *Very low birth weight (<1,500 g):* Begin PN promptly.
    *   *Critically ill:* Initiate PN when EN is unable to meet energy requirements for expenditure and growth.
*   **Pediatrics:**
    *   *Self-limited illness:* Delay PN for up to 7 days.
    *   *Infants:* Initiate PN within 1-3 days when EN is unable to provide sufficient nutrients.
    *   *Older children/adolescents:* Initiate PN within 4-5 days when EN is unable to provide sufficient nutrients.

### Indications for PN (Table 11-2)
*   **Impaired absorption or loss of nutrients via GI tract:**
    *   Massive small bowel resection (<120 cm small bowel distal to ligament of Treitz without colon; <60 cm with intact colon; neonates/infants unable to meet requirements).
    *   High output GI fistulae (>500 mL/day; location precludes enteral access).
    *   High volume fistula output with EN.
    *   Neutropenic colitis (typhlitis or opportunistic infection in immunocompromised patient).
    *   Small bowel mucosal disease (radiation/chemotherapy enteritis, autoimmune enteropathy, intractable diarrhea of infancy).
    *   Intestinal atresia, gastroschisis, volvulus, meconium ileus, necrotizing enterocolitis, mesenteric thrombosis.
*   **Mechanical bowel obstruction:** Stenosis/stricture, inflammatory disease, peritoneal carcinomatosis, severe adhesive disease, severe superior mesenteric artery syndrome.
*   **Restricted oral intake or EN necessary for bowel rest:**
    *   Ischemic bowel (mesenteric artery stenosis, abdominal compartment syndrome, low flow states).
    *   Severe pancreatitis (EN exacerbated pain, worsening serum lipase, infected phlegmon/pseudocyst, complex fistula, abdominal compartment syndrome).
    *   Chylous fistula (high output with low-fat diet/elemental formula).
    *   Preoperative status (severely malnourished with nonfunctional GI tract for 7-10 days prior to surgery).
*   **Motility disorders:** Prolonged ileus (diffuse peritonitis, medical treatment-induced), pseudo-obstruction, scleroderma, visceral organ myopathy, long segment Hirschsprung’s disease, severe adhesive disease.
*   **Inability to achieve/maintain enteral access or EN:** Hemodynamic instability, active GI bleeding, severe neutropenic fever, low birth weight infant.

*   **10% ILE:** 1.1 kcal/mL (4.6 kJ/mL)
*   **20% ILE:** 2 kcal/mL (8.4 kJ/mL)
*   **30% ILE:** 3 kcal/mL (12.6 kJ/mL) *(Note: 30% ILE approved only for TNA compounding, not direct IV administration).*
*   *Caloric contribution of dietary fat:* 9 kcal/g (38 kJ/g). ILE caloric density includes egg phospholipid emulsifier and glycerol.

## Routes of Administration
*   **Peripheral Parenteral Nutrition (PPN):**
    *   Indications: Mild-to-moderately stressed patients, expected GI return in 10-14 days, bridge therapy.
    *   Constraints: Requires lower concentrations (3%-5% amino acids, 5%-10% dextrose). Osmolarity limits generally 600-900 mOsm/L to prevent thrombophlebitis.
    *   Phlebitis mitigation: Subtherapeutic heparin (0.5-1 unit/mL), hydrocortisone (5 mg/L).
*   **Central Parenteral Nutrition (CPN):**
    *   Indications: Therapy >7-14 days, large nutrient requirements, poor peripheral access, fluid restriction, hypertonic solutions.

## Initiating and Advancing PN

### Adult Dosing
*   **Dextrose:** Max infusion rate 4-7 mg/kg/min. Routine care rarely exceeds 5 mg/kg/min.
*   **Lipid (ILE):** Max SO-ILE dose 2.5 g/kg/day or 60% of total daily calories. Recommended: 1 g/kg/day (not to exceed 30% of calories) to minimize negative effects of long-chain fatty acids. Mixed-oil ILE can be dosed up to 2.5 g/day.
*   **Test Dose:** Manufacturer guidelines recommend SO-ILE/SMOF test dose of 0.5-1 mL/min for 15-30 minutes (often deemed unnecessary in adults due to low incidence of hypersensitivity).

### Pediatric Dosing
*   **Dextrose:**
    *   *Premature infants:* Initiate at 5-6 mg/kg/min. Advance by 1%-2.5% or 2-4 mg/kg/min daily. Goal: 10-14 mg/kg/min (Max: 14-18 mg/kg/min).
    *   *Infants:* Goal 10-14 mg/kg/min.
    *   *Children:* Goal 8-10 mg/kg/min.
    *   *Adolescents:* Goal 5-6 mg/kg/min.
*   **Lipid (ILE):**
    *   Initiate at 0.5-1 g/kg/day. Advance by 0.5-1 g/kg/day.
    *   *Max SO-ILE:* 3 g/kg/day (infants), 2.5 g/kg/day (children). Max 60% total calories (neonates), 30% total calories (children).
    *   *Max FO-ILE:* 1 g/kg/day.
    *   *Infusion rate:* Infuse SO-ILE over 20-24 hours (do not exceed 1 g/kg in 4 hours). FO-ILE may be infused over 8-12 hours.
*   **Vitamins:**
    *   <2.5 kg: 2 mL/kg/day.
    *   $\ge$2.5 kg: 5 mL/day.
    *   *Alternative:* <1 kg (1.5 mL/day); 1-3 kg (3.25 mL/day); >3 kg up to 11 years (5 mL/day).
*   **Trace Elements:**
    *   <0.6 kg: 0.2 mL every other day.
    *   0.6 to <10 kg: 0.3 mL/kg/day (Max 1 mL/day).
    *   $\ge$50 kg: Adult dose (1 mL daily).

## Compounding, Stability, and Infection Control
*   **Filters:** 1.2-µm inline filter required for TNA, CAA-dextrose solutions, and co-infused ILE to remove particulates and *Candida albicans*.
*   **Tubing Changes (CDC Guidelines):** Every 24 hours for ILE (infused separately or in TNA). No more frequently than 96 hours (but at least every 7 days) for continuous IV solutions without ILE/blood.
*   **Calcium/Phosphate Precipitation Risk Mitigation:** Use calcium gluconate (less reactive than calcium chloride), add phosphate salts early in mixing sequence, add calcium last/nearly last, agitate mixture, maintain lower final pH (favored by higher dextrose/CAA and lower ILE concentrations).

## Complications of Parenteral Nutrition

### Parenteral Nutrition–Associated Liver Disease (PNALD)
*   **Presentation:** Elevated total bilirubin, AST, ALT, alkaline phosphatase. Direct bilirubin >2 mg/dL (34.2 µmol/L) in infants.
*   **Risk Factors:** Prematurity, sepsis, hypoxia, lack of EN, small bowel bacterial overgrowth, prolonged PN, excessive calories, SO-ILE phytosterols, choline/taurine deficiency.
*   **Management:** Reduce SO-ILE dose, use FO-ILE, cyclic PN, ursodiol, oral antibiotics (for bacterial overgrowth), maximize EN, avoid overfeeding.

### Hypertriglyceridemia
*   **Definition:** Triglycerides >400 mg/dL (4.52 mmol/L) in adults; >150-200 mg/dL (1.70-2.26 mmol/L) in preterm infants/neonates/pediatrics.
*   **Management:** Reduce ILE rate/dose or withhold ILE. Low-dose heparin (1 unit/mL) may stimulate lipoprotein lipase in neonates but carries compounding error risks.

### Hyperglycemia
*   **Target Range:** 140-180 mg/dL (7.8-10 mmol/L) in adults; <150 mg/dL (8.3 mmol/L) in neonates.
*   **Management:** Add 0.05-0.1 units regular insulin per gram of dextrose for mild hyperglycemia (140-180 mg/dL). Increase to 0.15-0.2 units/g dextrose for moderate hyperglycemia (181-200 mg/dL).

### Refeeding Syndrome
*   **Presentation:** Severe declines in serum phosphate, potassium, and magnesium; fluid retention.
*   **Management (Adults):** Initiate PN with 100-150 g dextrose or 10-20 kcal/kg for first 24 hours. Advance calories by 33% of goal every 1-2 days. Supplement thiamine 100 mg/day.
*   **Management (Pediatrics):** Initiate at 40%-50% of goal (dextrose 4-6 mg/kg/min). Advance dextrose by 1-2 mg/kg/min daily. Supplement thiamine 2 mg/kg/day (max 100-200 mg/day).

### Essential Fatty Acid Deficiency (EFAD)
*   **Prevention:** Provide 2%-4% of total calories as linoleic acid and 0.25%-0.5% as linolenic acid. (Approx. 100 g SO-ILE weekly or 200 g mixed-oil ILE weekly in adults).
*   **Diagnosis:** Triene-to-tetraene ratio > 0.4.
*   **Onset:** 2-4 weeks after lipid-free PN in adults; within 48 hours in newborn infants.

### Drug-Nutrient Interactions (Table 10-16)
| Medication | Effect |
| :--- | :--- |
| **ACE inhibitors / ARBs** | Increased urinary zinc losses |
| **Antacids** | Thiamine deficiency |
| **Antibiotics** | Vitamin K deficiency |
| **Aspirin** | Folic acid deficiency; increased vitamin C excretion |
| **Cathartics** | Increased requirements for vitamins D, C, and B$_6$ |
| **Cholestyramine / Colestipol** | Vitamins A, D, E, K and $\beta$-carotene malabsorption |
| **Corticosteroids** | Decreased vitamins A, D, and C |
| **Diuretics (loop)** | Thiamine deficiency |
| **Diuretics (thiazides)** | Increased urinary zinc losses |
| **Efavirenz** | Vitamin D deficiency |
| **H2 antagonists / PPIs** | Vitamin B$_{12}$ malabsorption (reduced acid impairs release from food); PPIs also decrease iron absorption |
| **Isoniazid** | Vitamin B$_6$ and niacin deficiency |
| **Isotretinoin** | Vitamin A increases toxicity |
| **Mercaptopurine** | Niacin deficiency |
| **Methotrexate** | Folic acid inhibits effect |
| **Orlistat** | Vitamins A, D, E, K malabsorption |
| **Pentamidine / Primidone / Trimethoprim** | Folic acid deficiency/depletion |
| **Phenobarbital / Phenytoin** | Increased vitamin D metabolism; Phenytoin also decreases folic acid |
| **Protease inhibitors** | Vitamin D deficiency (impaired renal hydroxylation) |
| **Sulfasalazine** | Folic acid malabsorption |
| **Warfarin** | Vitamin K inhibits effect; vitamins A, C, E may affect prothrombin time |
| **Valproic acid** | Zinc, carnitine deficiency |
| **Zidovudine** | Folic acid and B$_{12}$ deficiencies increase myelosuppression |

*Note: IV lipid emulsions used as vehicles for propofol (1.1 kcal/mL for 10%) and clevidipine (2.0 kcal/mL for 20%) contribute fat calories and must be accounted for to avoid overfeeding.*
